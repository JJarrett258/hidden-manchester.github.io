This tunnel used to take the Cheshire Lines Committee railway line under Chester Road and Talbot Road near to Throstles Nest Bridge. You can see the southern portal of this junction now if you sit on the left hand side of the tram (facing forwards) between Firswood and Trafford Bar, just as the tram heads under the Altrincham line and turns to join it.

It can be seen on Bartholemew’s 1900 map from Manchester Historical Maps. The Tunnel’s northern portal just beyond Boyer Street is covered (from around the 1980s) by the site of the Manchester Audi showroom, and is very near the former site of Greater Manchester Police. Part of the tunnel cuts under the corner of Henshaw’s Blind Asylum.

You can see other views of this tunnel on this OS map on the National Library of Scotland site and you can see an aerial photo taken form the south on Britain from Above. As with all other UK railways you can see the route on Rail Map Online.

A significant short section of the CLC ran from Cornbrook to Chorlton; via Throstle Nest South Junction then through a short tunnel, crossed under the MSJA line and Elsinore Road and continued in a cutting to Chorlton Junction. There it became the Midland line to Derby and Great Central line to Guide Bridge and Sheffield.
